First, a genealogy of the emergence of “Diversity” shows how this notion ultimately tamed earlier critical themes such as Cultural Exceptionalism. Second, an examination of the different theoretical tools developed in order to measure diversity reveals how this notion maintains an ideal of democracy against standardization (although not always explicitly). Third, the analysis of the recent mutations in cultural policies (with the pregnancy of notions such as “consumed diversity” and “discoverability”) is linked to the rise of cultural platforms and their recommendation algorithms.
