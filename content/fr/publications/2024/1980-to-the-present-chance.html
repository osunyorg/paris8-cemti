In this context, chance remains above all a powerful narrative mechanism in contemporary literature. A driver of encounters and deviations, the intervention of chance in the narrative gives rise to events that put an end to regularity and amplify individual potentialities. Conceived as a form, chance distorts the logic of narrative processes. Performed by contemporary narration, it adds extra layers to natural complexity and to the meanders of human decision-making, appearing not only as the necessary condition of modern humanity but also as an illustration of this complexity and of the barely predictable “chaotic processes” it engenders.
