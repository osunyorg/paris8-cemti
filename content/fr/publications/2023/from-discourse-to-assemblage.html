The first part presents the contribution of Michel Foucault's anti-essentialist approach to power relations, which enables us to break out of the double impasse that consists, on the one hand, in valuing all public expression of sexuality as a form of liberation and, on the other, in seeking to purify sexuality of all power relations. Four areas of research are then reviewed: the discursive analysis of media representations, the affect-based approach to reception surveys, approaches based on the materiality of technique and, finally, surveys of media production and regulation.
